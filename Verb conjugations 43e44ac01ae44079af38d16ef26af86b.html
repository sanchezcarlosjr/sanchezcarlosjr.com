Verb conjugations

Present Tense

  1. Simple Present
    • Affirmative: I walk, You walk, He/She/It walks, We walk, They walk
    • Negative: I do not (don't) walk, You do not (don't) walk, He/She/It does not (doesn't) walk, We do not (don't) walk, They do not (don't) walk
    • Question: Do I walk? Do you walk? Does he/she/it walk? Do we walk? Do they walk?
    • Emphatic: I do walk, You do walk, He/She/It does walk, We do walk, They do walk
  1. Present Continuous
    • Affirmative: I am walking, You are walking, He/She/It is walking, We are walking, They are walking
    • Negative: I am not (I'm not) walking, You are not (aren't) walking, He/She/It is not (isn't) walking, We are not (aren't) walking, They are not (aren't) walking
    • Question: Am I walking? Are you walking? Is he/she/it walking? Are we walking? Are they walking?
  1. Present Perfect
    • Affirmative: I have walked, You have walked, He/She/It has walked, We have walked, They have walked
    • Negative: I have not (haven't) walked, You have not (haven't) walked, He/She/It has not (hasn't) walked, We have not (haven't) walked, They have not (haven't) walked
    • Question: Have I walked? Have you walked? Has he/she/it walked? Have we walked? Have they walked?
  1. Present Perfect Continuous
    • Affirmative: I have been walking, You have been walking, He/She/It has been walking, We have been walking, They have been walking
    • Negative: I have not (haven't) been walking, You have not (haven't) been walking, He/She/It has not (hasn't) been walking, We have not (haven't) been walking, They have not (haven't) been walking
    • Question: Have I been walking? Have you been walking? Has he/she/it been walking? Have we been walking? Have they been walking?

Imperative Mood

  • Affirmative: Walk (you), Let's walk (we)
  • Negative: Do not (don't) walk (you), Let's not walk (we)

Subjunctive Mood

  1. Present Subjunctive

    • Question: (rarely used in modern English) Should I walk? Should you walk? Should he/she/it walk? Should we walk? Should they walk?

Voices

  1. Active Voice
    • Affirmative: I walk, I walked, I will walk
    • Negative: I do not (don't) walk, I did not (didn't) walk, I will not (won't) walk
    • Question: Do I walk? Did I walk? Will I walk?
  1. Passive Voice
    • Simple Present: I am walked, You are walked, He/She/It is walked, We are walked, They are walked
    • Simple Past: I was walked, You were walked, He/She/It was walked, We were walked, They were walked
    • Simple Future: I will be walked, You will be walked, He/She/It will be walked, We will be walked, They will be walked
    • Present Continuous: I am being walked, You are being walked, He/She/It is being walked, We are being walked, They are being walked
    • Past Continuous: I was being walked, You were being walked, He/She/It was being walked, We were being walked, They were being walked
    • Present Perfect: I have been walked, You have been walked, He/She/It has been walked, We have been walked, They have been walked
    • Past Perfect: I had been walked, You had been walked, He/She/It had been walked, We had been walked, They had been walked
    • Future Perfect: I will have been walked, You will have been walked, He/She/It will have been walked, We will have been walked, They will have been walked
    • Negative: I am not (I'm not) walked, I was not (wasn't) walked, I will not (won't) be walked
    • Question: Am I walked? Was I walked? Will I be walked?
