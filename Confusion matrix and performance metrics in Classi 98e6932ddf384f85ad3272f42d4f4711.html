F1 Score

We have different means such as Arithmetic mean, Geometric Mean, and Harmonic Mean which can be used to aggregate Recall and Precision into one metric, thus we evaluate model performance comprehensively. F1 Score, also known as balanced F-score or F-measure, is the Harmonic Mean of Recall and Precision and it serves as a helpful metric that considers both of them.

This definition helps us in Binary classification problems that is tasks where we have two classes However, what about Multi-class problems? We similarly aggregate the per-class F1 scores.

The Macro-averaged F1 score is computed by taking the arithmetic mean of all the per-class F1 scores which results in a penalization with the minority classes. This method treats all classes equally regardless of their support values.

The weighted-averaged F1 score is calculated using the mean and the support proportion as weight, therefore it favors the majority class. Support refers to the number of actual class occurrences in the dataset.

The micro average computes the proportion of correctly classified observations out of all observations, therefore it doesnâ€™t favor any class in particular. This definition yields the overall accuracy.

FAQ

Which average should we choose?

A micro F1 score aligns better with a balanced dataset. A macro average stands out in the context of an imbalanced dataset with equal importance to all classes. A weighted average proves preferable when the imbalanced dataset depends on the class contribution.
