Dependent prepositions

English associate some prepositions with verbs, they are dependent prepositions. My way to know if some prepositions apply is by searching in a dictionary and checking out the examples.

Suppose we look up “associate” on the Merriam-Webster website, the example is “They were closely associated with each other during the war.” so the verb “associate” is associated with “with”.

Answer some strange questions —to me

Q: How many Prolog programmers does it take to change a lightbulb?

A: “No”

Q: How many cars do you want in your video?

A: “Yes”

Explanation.

Some people might expect if you ask a number, you get back one, but you can answer “Yes” or “No”. “No” means none of them or as few as possible. “Yes” means all of them or as many as possible.
